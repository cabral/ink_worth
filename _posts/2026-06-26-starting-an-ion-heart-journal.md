This is the first entry. Before any play shows up, a note on what this blog is and how the entries work.

ION Heart is a solo tabletop game by Parable Games. You play a Pilot and a Mech, a companion that has outlived its previous pilots and remembers them. The setting is the Astral Union, a galaxy rebuilding after a war. You move through it in a series of Story Circuits: self-contained prompts that you play out and record in a journal as you go.

The writing is the game. There are no other players to perform for and no screen between the prompt and the page. You roll or draw to see what a circuit gives you, decide what your Pilot makes of it, and put that decision down in your own words. The record is the thing you keep.

I write the originals by hand, in ink, in a notebook. That is the real journal. What goes up here is a second pass: the same entries read back, trimmed, and typed so they make sense to someone who wasn't in the room. The handwritten draft keeps its mistakes. The version here is the edited one.

So the entries will read as a journal, because that is what they are. Some will be a single circuit. Some will cover a run of them. They won't add up to a tidy plot, because solo play rarely does. A Pilot and a Mech go somewhere, something happens, and one of them has to live with it.

The first real entry comes next. Until then the format is set: play a circuit, write it by hand, bring the edited version here.
