# Writing Style

Style guide for all prose in this repository. Any copy generated for the INK worth
site (page text, guides, descriptions, READMEs) must follow these rules. Source of
the catalog below: [tropes.fyi](https://tropes.fyi) by [ossama.is](https://ossama.is).

The hard rule for this project: **no em dashes anywhere.** Use commas, colons, or
parentheses for asides. Everything else below is the catalog of AI writing tells to avoid.

---

## Word Choice

### "Quietly" and Other Magic Adverbs
Overuse of "quietly" and similar adverbs to convey subtle importance. Also: "deeply",
"fundamentally", "remarkably", "arguably". Cut them when they exist only to make a dull
sentence feel important.

### "Delve" and Friends
Avoid "delve", "certainly", "utilize", "leverage" (verb), "robust", "streamline",
"harness". Use the plain word instead (use, strong, simplify, apply).

### "Tapestry" and "Landscape"
Avoid ornate nouns where simple ones work: "tapestry", "landscape", "paradigm",
"synergy", "ecosystem", "framework".

### The "Serves As" Dodge
Don't replace "is/are" with "serves as", "stands as", "marks", "represents". Just use
the plain copula.

## Sentence Structure

### Negative Parallelism
The "It's not X, it's Y" reframe. The single biggest tell. One per piece maximum; zero
is better. State the point directly. Includes the causal variant "not because X, but
because Y".

### "Not X. Not Y. Just Z."
The dramatic countdown. State the conclusion directly.

### "The X? A Y."
Self-posed rhetorical questions answered immediately. Don't ask questions nobody asked.

### Anaphora Abuse
Repeating the same sentence opening 3+ times. Vary the syntax.

### Tricolon Abuse
Overuse of rule-of-three. One is fine; back-to-back is not.

### "It's Worth Noting"
Cut filler transitions: "It's worth noting", "Importantly", "Interestingly", "Notably".

### Superficial Analyses
Avoid trailing "-ing" phrases that add false significance: "highlighting its
importance", "reflecting broader trends", "underscoring its role as...".

### False Ranges
"From X to Y" only when X and Y sit on a real spectrum with a meaningful middle.

### Gerund Fragment Litany
No streams of verbless "-ing" fragments after a claim. Integrate into a normal sentence.

## Paragraph Structure

### Short Punchy Fragments
Avoid manufactured emphasis through one-thought-per-line fragments. Vary paragraph length.

### Listicle in a Trench Coat
If it's a list, make it a list. Don't disguise "The first... The second... The third..."
as prose.

## Tone

### "Here's the Kicker"
No false suspense transitions: "Here's the kicker", "Here's the thing", "Here's where it
gets interesting".

### "Think of It As..."
Skip patronizing analogies unless an analogy was requested. The direct explanation is
usually clearer.

### "Imagine a World Where..."
No futurist invitations. Describe what exists or what you're proposing.

### False Vulnerability
No performative self-awareness or risk-free "honesty".

### "The Truth Is Simple"
Don't assert that something is simple or obvious. Show it.

### Grandiose Stakes Inflation
Most things are incremental and useful. Don't inflate to world-historical significance.

### "Let's Break This Down"
No pedagogical framing: "Let's unpack", "Let's explore", "Let's dive in". Just do it.

### Vague Attributions
Name the source or drop the claim. No "experts", "observers", "industry reports".

### Invented Concept Labels
Don't coin compound labels ("the supervision paradox", "the acceleration trap") and
present them as established terms.

## Formatting

### Em-Dash Addiction
For this repo: zero em dashes. Use commas and parentheses.

### Bold-First Bullets
Don't start every list item with a bolded keyword. Bold sparingly.

### Unicode Decoration
Use straight quotes and plain arrows in code. No curly quotes, no decorative arrows in prose.

## Composition

### Fractal Summaries
One pass through the content. Don't preview, then state, then recap at every level.

### The Dead Metaphor
Introduce a metaphor, use it, let it go. Don't ride it for ten paragraphs.

### Historical Analogy Stacking
One well-developed example beats four mentioned in passing.

### One-Point Dilution
If the point takes 400 words, write 400. Don't restate it eight ways.

### The Signposted Conclusion
No "In conclusion", "To sum up", "In summary". End when done.

### "Despite Its Challenges..."
Don't acknowledge problems as a ritual gesture before dismissing them.

---

Any single pattern used once may be fine. The problem is several together, or one
repeated. Write varied, specific, and plain.
